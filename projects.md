## About Studio Varaždin

**Studio Varaždin**, operating under the company **Lotus RC**, is a creative production studio founded and led by director **Timon Terzić**, based in Varaždin, Croatia. Over the past three years, the studio has established itself as northern Croatia's premier cinematic production house — specialising in documentary films, immersive physical experiences, music videos, tourism branding, and AI-augmented storytelling.

The studio's core philosophy has remained constant across every project: *story first, technology second*. Whether pointing a ZEISS lens at a sculptor's hands or engineering a sensory experience inside a wood-and-light elevator, every technical choice is in service of a human emotion. The studio operates bilingually in Croatian and English, works with drone aerials, high-end cinema glass, photogrammetry, Unreal Engine 5, and AI-generation tools — but it is the story architecture that separates Studio Varaždin from the field.

The client roster spans city tourist boards, national institutions, artists, universities, political campaigns, and commercial brands — all united by a need for content that moves people rather than merely informs them.

## 2023 — Foundations

### Špancirfest — 25th Anniversary Aftermovie
**Client:** Varaždin Tourist Board | **Type:** Festival Aftermovie | **Year:** 2023

Špancirfest is Croatia's largest street festival, held annually in the historic baroque centre of Varaždin. For its 25th anniversary edition, Studio Varaždin was commissioned to produce a cinematic aftermovie that would do justice to a decade of creative growth and celebrate the festival as a living, breathing character in its own right.

The film was conceived as a seven-minute multi-narrative experience. Rather than a conventional highlights reel, it was built around four interlocking human stories: a family searching for their lost child through the festival's many worlds; a young artist filling a sketchbook with scenes from the streets; a couple experiencing the festival's transformation from day to night; and a street musician's arc from pavement busker to main stage performer. The city of Varaždin itself acted as a fifth protagonist — introduced through an ambitious opening sequence where butterfly ornaments come to life and take flight through the old town in an FPV drone sequence.

Production involved a large multi-camera team shooting across ten festival days, capturing hyperlapses, drone lapses, Super 8mm-style footage, and intimate character moments. The edit was structured around a musical architecture — energy peaks, emotional pauses, rhythmic cuts — that transformed raw event footage into a deeply felt portrait of communal joy. The film culminated in a romantic night-sky shot from the medieval city walls, concert stage blazing below.

**Director:** Timon Terzić | **Camera Team:** Dario Mikulek, Dorian Domiter, Sven Danko, Ivica Hozmec, Luka Ivić, Stella Grbec | **Producer:** Hrvoje Selec

### Advent u Varaždinu — Christmas City Film
**Client:** Varaždin Tourist Board | **Type:** Destination Film | **Year:** 2023

Varaždin's multi-award-winning Christmas market has been recognised as one of the best advent destinations in Europe. Studio Varaždin produced a cinematic destination film for the 2023 edition, built around a tender, intimate narration written from a child's perspective — a letter to Santa Claus questioning whether magic is real."

The film employed a warm, amber-and-blue colour palette, lingering wide shots of the illuminated baroque city, and intimate close-ups of faces lit by candle and market glow. The narration — *"I know we will one day meet — I'll run after you and show mama and tata that Christmas magic exists"* — gave the film an emotional spine that elevated it beyond destination marketing into something resembling a personal memory. Produced to air across tourist board channels and regional broadcast, the film contributed to Varaždin's continued positioning as Croatia's premier Christmas destination.

## 2024 — Expansion

### VUDRAG — ZEISS Sessions: Maker Portrait
**Client:** Zeiss Industrial Quality Solutions | **Type:** Documentary Portrait Film | **Year:** 2024

One of the studio's most prestigious and internationally visible productions, this film was created in partnership with **Zeiss Industrial Quality Solutions** as part of their global *Maker Portrait* series — a documentary programme celebrating craftspeople whose work sits at the intersection of art and precision technology.

The subject was **Nikola Vudrag**, a Croatian sculptor working in metal — a man who transforms raw iron into living forms through a combination of physical force, technical mastery, and philosophical depth. The film follows Vudrag through his complete creative process: from early sketching sessions in a library surrounded by art books, through the fire-and-force of the metal workshop, and finally into the precision world of 3D scanning with Zeiss technology.

The film was structured in five acts: an ambient, reflective introduction; a deep dive into Vudrag's material philosophy (*"Metal is more than material — it's life forged in fire"*); the delicate process of scanning sculptures to preserve their finest details; the raw physicality of the workshop; and a contemplative finale in which Vudrag and his son Arthur approach a signature droplet sculpture — a moment of legacy, tradition, and the passing of craft from one generation to the next.

Shot with ZEISS cinema glass, the film demonstrated the studio's ability to bring international co-production standards to a Croatian-produced piece — and established a template for the studio's portrait filmmaking approach.

**Format:** ~3:20 min portrait documentary | **Deliverables:** Film + supporting content

### Advent Lift — Put do Kuće Djeda Mraza
**Client:** Varaždin Tourist Board | **Type:** Immersive Physical Experience | **Year:** 2024

Perhaps the most technically ambitious and conceptually original project in the studio's history, the **Advent Lift** was not a film — it was a world. A physical installation designed, scripted, engineered, and produced entirely by Studio Varaždin for the Advent u Varaždinu 2024 season.

The concept: visitors enter a custom-built, elevator-like structure (3.2 × 2.7m exterior; capacity 6–8 adults) and are transported on a three-minute immersive journey to the North Pole. The experience combined high-quality 5.1 spatial audio, synchronised LED lighting with programmable dimmer control, fog machines simulating flight through clouds, and a cinematic video presentation projected forward. The structure was controlled by a custom automation system featuring door sensors, a manual lever activation, and a three-button elf controller (Start / Stop / Reset).

The narrative was designed in moments: the cabin lifts off above Varaždin's old town at night; passing through narrow streets, it tears fairy lights off building facades and is struck by snowballs; a cat walks across the roof and leaps away; finally, the North Pole arrives in all its snow and silence. On arrival, guests ring a bell to open the exit doors.

The experience required Timon to coordinate electrical engineering, spatial audio production, set construction, programming, and narrative design simultaneously — making it one of the most cross-disciplinary productions in the studio's catalogue. The result was one of Advent u Varaždinu's most-shared and most-discussed attractions of the 2024 season.

**Format:** 3-minute live immersive experience | **Deliverables:** Experience design, video production, automation programming, set specifications

### Ivanec Film 2024 — Ivanec u Zagrljaju Ivanščice
**Client:** Grad Ivanec / HPD Ivančica | **Type:** Cinematic Destination Film | **Year:** 2024

Produced for the city of Ivanec and its mountaineering club, this film explored Ivanščica — the dramatic mountain ridge that defines the Ivanec region's character and skyline. Rather than a conventional promotional film, the production took a cinematic storytelling approach, framing the landscape through the perspective of a historian-detective uncovering layers of the city's deep past.

The script wove together multiple historical layers: Roman legions on ancient roads, Slavic forest mythology, medieval castle ruins, and the living community of modern Ivanec. Visual techniques included AI-generated matte paintings, orthomosaic archaeological reconstructions, weathered antique map overlays, and drone FPV shots from the mountain's most dramatic vantage points.

A key creative decision was the inclusion of fantasy sequences: a hiker encounters a luminous river spirit, attempts to reach her, and receives a crystal before she vanishes. A lightning bolt strikes a linden tree in a VFX-heavy sequence. The film concluded with a hyperlapse of the city at golden hour. Narration was written in a literary, poetic register — read by actor Kristijan Petrović, with music sourced from Artlist.io.

**Screenwriter:** Ljiljana Risek | **Credits read by:** Kristijan Petrović | **Format:** Cinematic short film

### Hrvatska Kovnica Novca — Croatian Mint
**Client:** Hrvatska Kovnica Novca (State-owned national mint) | **Type:** Institutional Promotional Film | **Year:** 2024

A prestige production for one of Croatia's most significant state institutions, the Croatian Mint commissioned Studio Varaždin to produce a flagship story video for their limited-edition coin collections — including Euro coins, commemorative series, indigenous species collections, and royal heritage coins.

The proposed format was a ~2-minute story film spanning three temporal acts: past, present, and future — reflecting the mint's role as guardian of national memory and monetary heritage. The production scope included original score composition, professional narration recording, visual effects and CGI for abstract coin-craft sequences, and additional vertical social media formats.

The project represented the studio's most institutionally prestigious engagement to date, requiring a tone of exacting gravitas, historical precision, and national pride — while remaining cinematically compelling and emotionally resonant for general audiences.

**Format:** ~2 min story film + social cut-downs | **Services:** Production, original score, VFX/CGI, narration

### Reformisti 2024 — Political Campaign Videos
**Client:** Reformisti (Political Party) | **Type:** Political Campaign Production | **Year:** 2024

Studio Varaždin produced a suite of thematic political campaign videos for the Reformisti party ahead of the 2024 Croatian parliamentary elections. The commission comprised four thematic videos (covering: pensioners and retirement homes; demography and education infrastructure; economy and EU funds; and transport infrastructure including rail and motorways) plus one call-to-vote video.

Each video featured a direct-to-camera format with supporting cutaway footage: drone aerials of relevant locations (POS housing developments, motorway corridors), repurposed archival photography, and footage produced specifically for the campaign including a local hairdresser salon and artisan workshop sequence. The approach required the studio to maintain a professional, values-driven tone while keeping content visually dynamic and accessible to a broad regional audience.

**Format:** 5 videos (4 thematic + 1 CTA) | **Production:** Studio / field mixed format

### Prva Gimnazija Varaždin — School Promotional Film
**Client:** Prva Gimnazija Varaždin | **Type:** Institutional Portrait Film | **Year:** 2024

A distinctive and inventive promotional film for Varaždin's oldest and most prestigious secondary school, this production followed the day of a student character, **Elizaveta**, as she moves through the school's various departments and encounters her exceptional classmates — each one a regional or national champion in their discipline: biology Olympians, competitive programmers, debaters, mathematicians, athletes, and literature students.

The film was structured as a time-reversal narrative: it opens with a chemistry experiment explosion, time freezes on Elizaveta's face, and the story rewinds to the beginning of her day — allowing the audience to experience the school's breadth through her eyes. Each subject was visually treated with its own cinematic language: the computer science room was lit entirely by the glow of a monitor; mathematics was visualised through hallucinatory formula overlays; the athletics track used parallax drone and gimbal work.

The finale revealed the entire film as having been directed by the school's own film club — an elegant meta-twist that looped back to the school's identity as a place that produces creators, not just achievers.

## 2025 — Ambition

### STEFANY — Otrov i Med (Music Video)
**Client:** Stefany (Pop/RnB Artist) | **Type:** Music Video | **Year:** 2025

The studio's most formally ambitious narrative film to date — a conceptually sophisticated music video for pop/RnB artist Stefany, built around a dual-world structure that dissects the anatomy of a toxic relationship.

The song *"Otrov i Med"* (Poison and Honey) provided the emotional blueprint. The film created two parallel reality streams for the same relationship: **The Bar** — glamorous, amber-lit, slow-motion seduction, shot in a film noir–meets–Chanel aesthetic with golden tones, dark marble, and neon highlights; and **The Red Studio** — a raw, brutally lit confessional space, bathed in full red wash, where the emotional truth behind the glamour was exposed through physical performance.

The two worlds were woven together through precise match-cuts: a hand on a cocktail glass cuts to a hand on a tear-stained face; a smile cuts to a scream; an embrace cuts to a body recoiling. By the final act, both worlds had fully collapsed into each other — the bar emptied, the cocktatil spilled, and Stefany stood alone in the red circle of light, looking directly at camera.

The film pushed the studio's colour design and camera choreography to new levels, with differentiated visual languages (fluid dolly and bokeh for the bar; static, high-contrast, circular movement for the studio) that required a high degree of pre-visualisation and precision on set.

**Format:** ~3:07 min music video | **Visual worlds:** Bar (illusion) + Red Studio (truth)

### Trakostyan Tours — Glasnik Vječnosti (Messenger of Eternity)
**Client:** Trakostyan-Tours (Tourism Agency) | **Type:** Historical Cinematic Short Film | **Year:** 2025

One of the studio's most ambitious and narratively complex productions — a two-minute cinematic film commissioned by tourism agency Trakostyan-Tours as both an artistic statement and their central marketing vehicle.

*"Glasnik Vječnosti" (Messenger of Eternity)* weaves three historical periods into a single tapestry, using a falcon as the eternal witness who moves between eras. The three acts unfold across three landmark locations in northern Croatia:

- **Act I – Roman Era (Varaždinske Toplice):** A legionnaire, exhausted from battle, approaches sacred waters seeking guidance. The goddess Minerva awakens from her sleep in the ruins of the Roman spa complex. A forest nymph gallops through ancient woodland on a white horse. The falcon carries the thread of fate between them.
- **Act II – Medieval Era (Trakošćan Castle):** Barbara Celjska — the "Black Queen" of medieval legend — schemes in candlelit chambers. A raven delivers a sealed letter. Vlad Tepeš (Dracula), member of the Order of the Dragon, lurks in shadow. A castle messenger rushes through corridors, connecting conspiracies.
- **Act III – Baroque Era (Varaždin Old Town):** Empress Maria Theresa and Baron Franjo Trenk exchange secret correspondence in the regal baroque streets. A falconer in court dress carries the message forward through time.

The production required full period costuming for over fifteen performers, location shooting across four sites, a trained live falcon and falconer, fog machines, period props, and a rigorous shoot-list across three consecutive production days. The film was the centrepiece of Trakostyan-Tours' tourism offering and was accompanied by a 15-slide presentation designed as "an explorer's leather-bound notebook."

**Tone:** Game of Thrones meets Croatian baroque heritage | **Format:** ~2 min cinematic promotional short

### 100 Dvoraca Sjevera Hrvatske — Film Script & Concept
**Client:** Castle Network of Northern Croatia | **Type:** Cinematic Drama / Tourism Film | **Year:** 2025 (In Progress)

A fairytale-structured cinematic short film that tells the love stories and tragedies hidden inside four of northern Croatia's most iconic castles — Trakošćan, Veliki Tabor, Stari Grad Varaždin, and Stari Grad Čakovec — through the eyes of a ten-year-old girl named **Ema** and her mother.

The film's frame narrative is intimate and warm: a mother finds her daughter reading a forbidden magical pop-up book under the covers late at night. Instead of scolding her, she climbs into bed and reads with her — and together, they journey into the legends of each castle. The pop-up book becomes a portal: paper knights ride into battle, medieval lovers are reunited in paper-and-cardboard set pieces, and one tragic love story leaves Ema briefly in tears before her mother turns the page.

Four love stories unfold across the castles:
- **Trakošćan:** A knight leaves for war; his young wife watches from the window, uncertain he'll return.
- **Veliki Tabor:** The legendary romance of Fridrik Frankopan and Veronika — a feudal lord falls from his horse and is nursed back to health by a peasant girl; they fall in love despite the impossible distance of their stations.
- **Stari Grad Varaždin:** Officer Milić and young Stazica, whose love keeps arriving and being taken away by time — each scene, Milić appears older, until the final moment when Stazica vanishes just before their reunion.
- **Stari Grad Čakovec:** The playful, human portrait of Petar Zrinski and Katarina — a man of war and a woman of grace, watching their young son Ivan try to defeat his father in the mud of the castle courtyard.

The film is designed to work on multiple levels: as a cinematic attraction for families visiting the castles, as a premium souvenir pop-up book, and as a flagship piece of cultural heritage promotion for the northern Croatian tourism network.

**Status:** In production (script complete, pre-production active) | **Format:** Cinematic short film + pop-up book companion
